# Indicators and Practices for Assessing Cybersecurity Awareness in Organisations

A Systematic Literature Review of how organisations measure cybersecurity awareness (CSA), conducted at **Jönköping University, School of Engineering**, June 2026.

> **Authors:** Sahala Kallan Mulleri, Mohamed Ibrahim
>
> **Supervisor:** Erik Bergström · **Examiner:** Joakim Kävrestad · **Scope:** 15 ECTS

[Please Read the full thesis (PDF)](Mastersthesis.pdf)

## Summary

Cybersecurity awareness is a central part of most organisations' security strategy, but how it is *measured* is unclear and inconsistent across the literature. This SLR identifies the indicators, measurement approaches, and CSA dimensions used in existing research, and highlights the gap in SME-focused work.

## Methodology

PRISMA-based SLR across four academic databases (IEEE Xplore, Scopus, SpringerLink, ACM Digital Library). After applying the inclusion and exclusion criteria, **52 studies published between 2010 and 2026** were selected for analysis.

## Research questions

1. What indicators are used by organisations to measure cybersecurity awareness?
2. What measurement approaches are used by these indicators?
3. What dimensions of CSA are measured in the literature?
4. To what extent does the existing literature address CSA measurement in SME contexts, and what indicators are used there?

## Key findings

- CSA is measured using a mix of **self-reported, knowledge-based, behavioural, and organisational** indicators. Self-reported questionnaires and surveys dominate; behavioural signals like phishing-simulation outcomes and incident-reporting rates appear much less often.
- **Ten recurring CSA dimensions** were identified. Email use / phishing susceptibility, password management, and information handling receive the most attention. Cloud-service use and workstation security receive comparatively little.
- **SME-focused CSA measurement research is substantially under-represented**, despite SMEs facing real resource limits and rising threat exposure. This is the clearest research gap.
